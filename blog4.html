WoeBot

WoeBot is a therapy chatbot that advertises itself to use evidence-based approaches to reduce stress of the user and guide the user to understand more about themselves. 

Approach effectiveness

Early results from research teams at Stanford University have shown that college students who chatted with Woebot significantly reduced the symptoms of depression in two weeks.11 However, this is only a short term study, and a lot of studies on Woebot happen to be funded by the parent company of Woebot itself, which may be a cause for concern.12 Nonetheless, Woebot is perhaps the single most researched backed mental health app, and that gives it a lot of points. Woebot is one of the only apps that use cognitive behavioral therapy to treat it’s users of depression. CBT happens to be the most effective treatment for anxiety and depression, and arguably the most widely studied form of psychotherapy.13 Furthermore, users, reviewers and journalists alike have presented a mostly positive opinion on this app.

We give Woebot a functionality effectiveness rating of 9 out of 10.

Functionality

Woebot uses a combination of natural language processing (NLP), evidence based collection of stories and professional psychotherapy to deliver user experience. There are clearly still a lot of ways for the service to improve, as this field of service is still relatively young. 

We give Woebot a functionality rating of 6 out of 10.

Security

Woebot sends a somewhat contradictory message when it comes to privacy, Woebot collects a lot of personal information, and that this information can be shared with insurance companies and can share data with facebook, especially when being used through facebook’s messenger app.14 Woebot yet claims that “We never, ever sell or share your data with advertisers”. Woebot does share anonymized data, but this is also a problem according to article 'Anonymised' data can never be totally anonymous.15

We give Woebot a security rating of 5 out of 10.

Affordability

As of now, Woebot is completely free. Although Woebot is currently attempting to have its services approved by the FDA and gain revenue from prescription therapy. 

We give it an affordability ranking of 10 out of 10.
